## Brand & Style

This design system is built on the principles of **Clinical Modernism**. The goal is to instill immediate confidence in the user by mirroring the cleanliness and precision of a high-end medical facility. The brand personality is authoritative yet empathetic, utilizing a vast amount of whitespace to reduce cognitive load and anxiety—essential for a healthcare environment.

The aesthetic leans heavily into **Minimalism** with a **Corporate/Modern** structure. It prioritizes clarity over decoration. Surfaces are primarily stark white or extremely soft blue to evoke a sterilized, safe environment. Interaction patterns are predictable and stable, reinforcing the feeling of professional reliability. High-contrast typography and generous touch targets ensure the application remains accessible to users across varying degrees of health literacy and physical ability.

## Colors

The palette is anchored by "Clinical Blue" (#0052CC), a deep, trustworthy primary that symbolizes medical expertise. The secondary blue (#E6F0FF) is used for large-scale surfaces and backgrounds to provide a calming, low-glare alternative to pure white.

- **Primary:** Used for primary actions, branding, and active states.
- **Surface - Clinical Blue:** Used for information cards, subtle highlights, and section differentiation.
- **Semantic Palette:** Success (green), Warning (amber), and Error (red) follow standard medical conventions for triage and status updates, ensuring high visibility without causing unnecessary alarm.
- **Neutral:** A deep navy-tinted charcoal (#091E42) is used for text to maintain high legibility while appearing softer and more professional than pure black.

## Typography

Typography in this design system is driven by **Manrope** for structural headings and **Atkinson Hyperlegible Next** for all functional and body text. 

- **Manrope** provides a geometric, modern, and high-tech feel for headlines, signaling the "AI" aspect of the app.
- **Atkinson Hyperlegible Next** is selected specifically for its medical-grade accessibility. Its distinctive character shapes prevent misreading—a critical requirement when displaying lab results or dosage instructions.

For mobile devices, `headline-lg` should automatically scale down to `headline-lg-mobile` to prevent awkward line breaks. All body copy maintains a generous line height to ensure ease of reading for users who may be stressed or visually impaired.

## Layout & Spacing

This design system employs a **Fluid Grid** model with a base-4 rhythm. On mobile, the system uses a 4-column layout with 20px side margins. On desktop, it expands to a 12-column centered layout with a max-width of 1200px.

Spacing is used to create "clinical zones." High-priority information is isolated with `xl` (32px) padding, while related data points are grouped with `sm` (8px) gaps. Content should never feel cramped; if in doubt, increase the whitespace to emphasize the "clean" medical aesthetic.

## Elevation & Depth

To maintain a sterilized, flat medical feel, this design system avoids heavy shadows. Instead, it utilizes **Tonal Layers** and **Low-Contrast Outlines**.

- **Level 0 (Base):** Pure white background (#FFFFFF).
- **Level 1 (Cards/Surfaces):** A subtle 1px border (#DFE1E6) or a light blue tint (#E6F0FF).
- **Level 2 (Active/Floating):** An extremely soft, diffused blue-tinted shadow (0px 4px 12px rgba(0, 82, 204, 0.08)) is used only for elements that require immediate user attention, like a diagnostic modal or an AI scanning sheet.
- **Clinical Surfaces:** For data-heavy sections, use the `clinical_muted` (#F4F5F7) background to visually separate information from the main app shell.

## Shapes

The shape language is **Rounded** (8px / 0.5rem base radius). This specific radius is used to soften the "sharpness" of medical data without making the app feel overly casual or "bubbly." 

- **Small Components (Buttons, Inputs):** 8px radius.
- **Large Components (Cards, Modals):** 16px (1rem) radius.
- **Contextual Chips:** 24px (1.5rem) radius for a pill-shaped appearance to differentiate them from actionable buttons.

## Components

### Buttons
Primary buttons use the Primary Blue (#0052CC) with white text. Secondary buttons use a transparent background with a Primary Blue outline. All buttons have a minimum height of 48px to ensure accessibility for all users.

### Input Fields
Inputs use a white background with a 1px neutral border. Upon focus, the border thickens to 2px Primary Blue and adds a soft blue glow. Error states must include both a red border (#FF5630) and an icon for accessibility.

### Clinical Data Cards
These are the primary vehicle for AI scan results. They should use the Clinical Blue surface (#E6F0FF) with a slightly darker blue border. Use `headline-md` for the title and `body-sm` for the timestamp of the scan.

### Status Chips
Chips are used for triage status (e.g., "Normal", "Requires Attention", "Urgent"). They should use a low-opacity background of the semantic color (e.g., Success Green at 10% opacity) with high-contrast text of the same hue for maximum legibility.

### AI Progress Indicator
A custom component for this design system: a smooth, pulsing light-blue bar or ring that appears while the AI is analyzing images. It should use the Tertiary Color (#00B8D9) to distinguish "process" from "final results."
